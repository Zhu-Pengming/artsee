## Additional Sections

### Program Structure & Offerings

#### Undergraduate Programs
- **BFA in Art**
  - **Description**: A professional degree with eight concentrations, including Graphic Design, Illustration/Animation, Painting/Drawing, Photography/Video, Printmaking, Sculpture/Ceramics, and Industrial Design.
  - **Admission Requirements**: A SlideRoom portfolio of 15–20 pieces, demonstrating independent thinking and diverse experiences. At least 10 drawings from direct observation if drawing is the focus.
- **BA in Art**
  - **Description**: A broader liberal-arts framing with no concentration declaration required.
  - **Admission Requirements**: No portfolio required.
- **BA in Art History**
  - **Description**: A separate track with areas of study spanning ancient/medieval/Renaissance, 19th–21st century, and "global perspectives."
  - **Admission Requirements**: No portfolio required.

#### Graduate Programs
- **MFA in Studio Art**
  - **Description**: A three-year residency program with a focus on studio art. The program culminates in an NYC gallery exhibition and a thesis show at the William Benton Museum of Art.
  - **Admission Requirements**: A portfolio via UConn SlideRoom, no GRE required, and a January 15 deadline.
- **MFA in Digital Media Design**
  - **Description**: A related program offered by a different department within the School of Fine Arts.
  - **Admission Requirements**: Application through the UConn Graduate School online application, portfolio via UConn SlideRoom, and a January 11 deadline for funding consideration.

### Admissions Requirements

#### Undergraduate
- **Application Process**
  - **Common Application or Coalition Application**: The application is submitted through one of these platforms.
  - **Application Fee**: $80 (non-refundable).
  - **Test Scores**: The university is test-optional through Fall 2026. Submitted SAT middle 50% ≈ 1210–1420, ACT ≈ 29–33.
  - **English Proficiency**: Minimum TOEFL 79 / IELTS 6.5 / Duolingo 100 / PTE 60.
  - **GPA**: Minimum 3.0 in previous study; average for Storrs-admitted students ≈ 3.5.
  - **Acceptance Rate**: 52% for the Class of 2028.
  - **Portfolio**: Required for BFA applicants, submitted via SlideRoom.
  - **Deadlines**: Early Decision: November 1 (with SlideRoom portfolio also due Nov 1); Priority Deadline: December 1; Regular Decision / International first-year: January 15; Transfer: February 1; Spring admission: October 1.

#### Graduate
- **Application Process**
  - **UConn Graduate School online application**: The application is submitted through the university's online application system.
  - **Application Fee**: $80 international.
  - **GRE**: Not required for MFA.
  - **TOEFL/IELTS/Duolingo**: Required for non-native English speakers.
  - **Portfolio**: Submitted through UConn Graduate SlideRoom.
  - **Deadline**: January 15 (for Fall start).
  - **Admit Yield**: Up to 5 highly qualified applicants per year.
