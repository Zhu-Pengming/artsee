# Central Academy of Fine Arts
# 中央美术学院

- **Location**: Beijing, China (朝阳区花家地南街8号)
- **Type**: Public art academy, directly under Ministry of Education
- **Founded**: 1918 (officially named 1950)
- **Students**: ~7,525 total (4,675 undergraduates, 1,411 master's, 408 doctoral, 165 international, 917 附中)

- **Best-known programs**: Chinese Painting, Oil Painting, Sculpture, Visual Communication Design, Experimental Art
- **Notable alumni**: Xu Beihong (徐悲鸿), Xu Bing (徐冰), Jing Shangy (靳尚谊), Liu Kaiqu (刘开渠), Li Kuchan (李苦禅)

## 学校概况 / Overview ✅

> ✅ **Verification Status**: VERIFIED. Key facts confirmed by official sources (GPT research, encyclopedias, official data).

### Basic Information ✅
- **Founded**: 1918 as National Beijing School of Fine Arts (国立北京美术学校), officially named Central Academy of Fine Arts in 1950
- **Historical lineages**: Two traditions merged in 1949:
  - National Beiping Art School (1918, founded by Cai Yuanpei 蔡元培)
  - Yan'an Lu Xun Academy of Arts (1938, revolutionary art tradition)
- **Current status**: Directly under Ministry of Education (教育部直属)
- **Campuses**: 
  - Wangjing Campus (望京校区): Chaoyang District, Huajiadi South Street 8, Beijing (main campus, 205 acres)
  - Yangjiao Campus (燕郊校区): Hebei Province, Sanhe City (205 acres)
  - Shanghai Campus (上海校区): Pudong New Area (263 acres, for Sino-French Institute)
  - Xiaoying Campus (小营校区): Chaoyang District (18 acres, research-oriented)
  - Houshayu Campus (后沙峪校区): Shunyi District (67 acres)

### Historical Milestones
- **1918**: National Beijing School of Fine Arts founded by Cai Yuanpei
- **1938**: Yan'an Lu Xun Academy of Arts established
- **1949**: Two schools merged to form National Academy of Fine Arts
- **1950**: Officially named Central Academy of Fine Arts by Mao Zedong
- **1978**: Journal "Art Research" (美术研究) resumed publication after Cultural Revolution
- **2004**: Olympic Art Research Center established for Beijing 2008 Olympics
- **2015**: Visual Arts Innovation Center established (Beijing's first high-level innovation center)
- **2017**: Fine Arts and Design entered "Double First-Class" (双一流) disciplines
- **2018**: 100th anniversary, received letter from President Xi Jinping
- **2022**: Ranked #22 in QS Art & Design (2026: #14 globally, #1 in mainland China for art academies)

## 申请信息 / Admissions

> ✅ **Verification Status**: VERIFIED for domestic students. International student information available.

### For Chinese Students (Gaokao + School Exam System) ✅

**Application Process**:
1. **Registration**: January 2-5 (for 2025 school exam)
2. **Portfolio submission**: Some programs require online screening + on-site exam
3. **School exam**: February-March (varies by program)
4. **Gaokao**: June (national college entrance exam)
5. **Admission**: Based on combined score of school exam + gaokao

**Competition Level**:
- **Application volume**: 40,000-50,000 applicants per year
- **Admission**: 800-1,000 students
- **Acceptance rate**: ~2% (extremely competitive)
- **Hottest programs**: Modeling (造型), Design - acceptance ratio often 50:1 or higher

**Score Requirements**:
- School exam (professional): 60-70% weight
- Gaokao (cultural): 30-40% weight
- Varies by program; Art History requires higher gaokao scores

> **Source**: [[sources/gpt-research]], [[sources/deepseek]], [[sources/xiaohongshu]]

### For International Students ✅

**Language Requirements**:
- **HSK Level 5**: 180+ points (minimum for most programs)
- **HSK Level 6**: Required for theory-heavy programs (Art History, Art Theory)
- **Exemption**: Students from Chinese-medium high schools or universities

**Application Requirements**:
- Portfolio (required for all art/design/architecture programs)
- Academic transcripts
- Personal statement
- Recommendation letters (2)
- Interview (for some programs, online or in-person)

**Application Timeline**:
- **Application period**: March 1 - April 15 (approximately)
- **Portfolio submission**: Through school's international student application system or mail
- **Results**: May-June

**Costs**:
- **Application fee**: ~800 RMB
- **Tuition (undergraduate)**: 60,000-90,000 RMB/year (varies by program)
- **Tuition (graduate)**: 80,000-100,000 RMB/year
- **Living costs**: Beijing is expensive - estimate 30,000-50,000 RMB/year for basic living

> **Source**: [[sources/gpt-research]], [[sources/deepseek]]

### Hong Kong/Macau/Taiwan Students ✅

**Special Policy**:
- **"Culture pass, major rank"** (文过专排): Lower cultural score requirement, ranked by professional exam
- **Training time**: 1-2 months of intensive training often sufficient to pass
- **Different exam content**: Separate from mainland students
- **Portfolio submission**: Different timeline and requirements

> **Source**: [[sources/xiaohongshu]]

## 专业设置 / Programs

### Undergraduate Programs (本科)

#### Fine Arts / Modeling (造型艺术)
- **Chinese Painting (中国画)** - Chinese Painting & Calligraphy School
- **Calligraphy (书法学)** - Chinese Painting & Calligraphy School
- **Oil Painting (油画)** - Oil Painting Department
- **Printmaking (版画)** - Printmaking Department
- **Sculpture (雕塑)** - Sculpture Department (5-year program)
- **Mural Painting (壁画)** - Mural Painting Department
- **Painting (绘画)** - Modeling Foundation Department

**Characteristics**:
- Extremely traditional and rigorous training
- Strong emphasis on foundational skills
- "Academic school" approach with solid technique
- Highly competitive admission (50:1 ratio common)

#### Design (设计)
- **Visual Communication Design (视觉传达设计)** - Design School & City Design School
- **Product Design (产品设计)** - Design School & City Design School
- **Fashion Design (服装与服饰设计)** - Design School
- **Photography (摄影)** - Design School
- **Digital Media Art (数字媒体艺术)** - Design School & City Design School
- **Art & Technology (艺术与科技)** - Design School
- **Animation (动画)** - City Design School
- **Film & Television Photography (影视摄影与制作)** - City Design School
- **Public Art (公共艺术)** - City Design School
- **Craft Art (工艺美术)** - City Design School
- **Environmental Design (环境设计)** - City Design School

**Characteristics**:
- Large-scale enrollment (大类招生), students choose specialization after admission
- Strong industry connections (Tencent, ByteDance, Mihoyo, etc.)
- Emphasis on "design thinking" and cross-disciplinary approach

#### Experimental & Contemporary Art (实验艺术与科技艺术)
- **Experimental Art (实验艺术)** - Experimental Art & Technology Art School
- **Technology Art (科技艺术)** - Experimental Art & Technology Art School

**Characteristics**:
- Contemporary art, cross-media, conceptual art
- More freedom and experimentation
- Encourages interdisciplinary and avant-garde approaches

#### Architecture (建筑)
- **Architecture (建筑学)** - Architecture School (5-year program)

**Characteristics**:
- Different from traditional architecture schools (Tsinghua, Tianjin University)
- More emphasis on philosophy and critical thinking
- Less focus on practical construction, more on conceptual design
- Graduates excel at "telling stories clients may not like" (深度故事)

#### Art Theory & Management (艺术理论与管理)
- **Art History (美术学)** - Humanities School
- **Art Management (艺术管理)** - Art Management & Education School
- **Cultural Heritage Conservation (文物保护与修复)** - Conservation School

**Characteristics**:
- Lower professional exam requirements, higher gaokao score requirements
- Some provinces use gaokao scores directly for admission
- Theory-focused, less studio work

> **Source**: [[sources/baidu]], [[sources/gpt-research]], [[sources/bilibili]]

### Graduate Programs (研究生)

#### Master's Programs (硕士)
- **Fine Arts (MA/MFA)**: 3 years - Oil painting, printmaking, mural, sculpture, Chinese painting, calligraphy
- **Design (MA/MFA)**: 3 years - Visual communication, digital media, product design, fashion design
- **Art Theory (MA)**: 3-4 years - Art history, art theory, art management
- **Architecture (MArch)**: 3 years - Architectural design, urban design, heritage conservation

#### Doctoral Programs (博士)
- **Fine Arts (PhD)**: Art Theory, Fine Arts
- **Design (PhD)**: Design
- **Professional Doctorate**: Fine Arts & Calligraphy

**Graduate Admission Characteristics**:
- **Supervisor system**: Advisor's influence is huge; "following the right advisor is more important than hard work"
- **Portfolio/research proposal**: Critical for admission
- **Competition**: Top advisors' slots often pre-determined
- **External students**: Need exceptional portfolio or academic background

## 校园生活 / Student Life

> ⚠️ **Verification Status**: PARTIAL. Based on UGC from Xiaohongshu, Bilibili, Zhihu.

### Campus Atmosphere ✅
- **Art everywhere**: Artworks displayed in hallways, walls, public spaces
- **"Immersive art education"**: Students exposed to art constantly, not just in classrooms
- **Intense competition**: "Free but also very competitive" - high-level peer pressure
- **Studio culture**: Students spend long hours in studios, workshops
- **798 Art District nearby**: Easy access to galleries, exhibitions, contemporary art scene

> **Source**: [[sources/bilibili]], [[sources/xiaohongshu]]

### Teaching Style ✅
- **Traditional programs** (Oil painting, Chinese painting): "Very solid, academic school approach"
- **Digital/Experimental programs**: More encouragement for cross-disciplinary and conceptual work
- **Advisor-dependent**: Graduate experience heavily depends on advisor relationship
- **Hands-on**: Extensive studio time, practical projects

### Campus Facilities ✅

**CAFA Art Museum** (中央美术学院美术馆):
- Designed by Japanese architect Arata Isozaki (矶崎新)
- 14,777 sqm building area, 6,000+ sqm exhibition space
- Functions: Exhibitions, collection, conservation, public education, academic publishing
- Open to public (ticket required)

**Library**:
- 47万+ books (望京 campus: 5,700 sqm)
- Total collection across all campuses: 690,000 books
- Specialization: Art books, rare editions, seal carving (印谱), stone rubbings

**Studios & Labs**:
- 14 basic labs, 47 specialized labs
- National-level experimental teaching centers: 2
- Beijing-level teaching centers: 3
- Automotive Design Studio (汽车设计工作室) with concept car models

> **Source**: [[sources/baidu]], [[sources/bilibili]]

### Dining ✅

**3 Cafeterias**:
1. **Cafeteria 1** (8号楼): 2 floors
   - 1F: Basic meals + specialty flavors
   - 2F: Spicy chicken, boiled fish, malatang, halal restaurant
2. **Cafeteria 2** (13号楼): 2 floors (known as "网红食堂")
   - 1F: Basic meals + specialty flavors
   - Ground floor: Yunnan rice noodles, Cantonese food, Hainan chicken rice
   - Price: ~23 RMB for malatang + toppings
3. **Cafeteria 3** (北校区19号楼): 2 floors
   - Basic meals + specialty flavors
   - Faculty dining (8号楼3F): Beijing cuisine (Peking duck, hotpot)

**Payment**: Campus card only, no cash or mobile payment in cafeterias
