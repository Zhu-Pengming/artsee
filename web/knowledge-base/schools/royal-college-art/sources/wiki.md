# Royal College of Art (RCA)

## TL;DR
The Royal College of Art (RCA) is a prestigious public research university in London, UK, established in 1837. It is renowned for its excellence in art and design education, consistently ranking first in the world for art and design in QS World University Rankings. RCA offers a range of postgraduate and doctoral programs across various disciplines, with a strong emphasis on practice-based learning. The college boasts a rich history of influential alumni and strong industry connections.

## Key Claims
- **Establishment and History**: RCA was founded in 1837 as the Government School of Design, later renamed to the Royal College of Art in 1896. It has a history of over 180 years and has been at the forefront of art and design education in the UK.
- **Campus and Location**: The college has three campuses in London: South Kensington, Battersea, and White City. Each campus is equipped with state-of-the-art facilities and studios.
- **Academic Programs**: RCA offers a diverse range of postgraduate and doctoral programs across art, design, and engineering disciplines. The college is known for its practice-based learning approach and strong industry connections.
- **Academic Reputation**: RCA consistently ranks first in the world for art and design in QS World University Rankings, reflecting its high-quality education and research.
- **Alumni**: The college has a strong network of influential alumni, including notable designers, artists, and entrepreneurs like Jony Ive, Thomas Heatherwick, and David Hockney.
- **Industry Connections**: RCA maintains strong relationships with leading companies and organizations in the art and design industry, providing students with valuable opportunities for internships and collaborations.
