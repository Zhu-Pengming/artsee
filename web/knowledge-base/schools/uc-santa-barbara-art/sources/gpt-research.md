## 一、项目基本信息与学位结构

### 1.1 院系归属与学位地图

The Department of Art at UCSB is part of the College of Letters and Science (L&S) and falls under the Division of Humanities and Fine Arts. Additionally, there is an independent College of Creative Studies (CCS) Art program. The BA in Art is offered through both L&S and CCS, with the CCS path being described as the "undergraduate version of a graduate school." It's important to note that UCSB's Studio Art program does not offer a BFA degree, and the L&S BFA is typically granted in Music and other disciplines.

### 1.2 研究生 MFA in Art

The MFA in Art at UCSB is a two-year, full-time residency program requiring a minimum of 72 quarter units. The program structure includes a mix of studio courses, theory and criticism courses, thesis preparation, and elective courses. The first-year spring cohort engages in a group exhibition and presentation, and students are evaluated by the faculty to determine if they will proceed to the second year. A thesis committee of three (up to four) faculty members is formed before the fourth quarter, and students complete a thesis project, a 25-page written thesis, and a graduate exhibition at the AD&A Museum.

### 1.3 规模与师生比

The MFA program at UCSB has a small, selective admissions process with approximately 6 new students admitted each year, 10-14 students in residence, and 90-100 applications per year. This results in an estimated acceptance rate of 6-7%. The Department of Art has approximately 12-14 ladder faculty members, and the MFA student-to-faculty ratio is approximately 1:1, reflecting the intensive studio environment and close mentorship.

## 二、申请要求与作品集要求

### 2.1 本科申请

The BA in Art at UCSB is offered through the UC Application system and does not require a portfolio for admission. Students are admitted based on their performance in three introductory courses (Art 1A, 1C, and 7A-D series). For students seeking a change of major into the Art program, a portfolio is required, consisting of 15-20 pieces of work compiled into a single PDF, along with an image list.

### 2.2 MFA 申请

The MFA application process involves two systems: the UCSB Online Graduate Division Application and SlideRoom for submitting the portfolio and additional materials. The portfolio should include 10-20 files, and each piece should be accompanied by a title, date, dimensions, medium, and a brief description. The statement of purpose and personal history statement are also required, along with three letters of recommendation and a CV/Resume.

### 2.3 国际学生额外要求

International students must meet specific language requirements, including TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo scores. The specific requirements may vary depending on the level of study and the program.

## 三、师资力量

The faculty at the Department of Art and CCS Art is diverse and includes renowned artists and scholars in various fields of contemporary art. The faculty members are actively involved in research, teaching, and public engagement, providing a rich learning environment for students.

## 四、课程设置与工作室设施

The curriculum at UCSB includes a range of courses and studio facilities that support students in developing their skills and exploring various artistic disciplines. The MFA program offers a comprehensive range of studio courses, theory and criticism courses, and elective courses. The studios and facilities are well-equipped to meet the needs of students working in different media.

## 五、学费、奖学金与录取数据

The cost of attendance at UCSB varies depending on the program and the student's residency status. The MFA program offers full funding through tuition/fee remission, stipend, and TA employment. The acceptance rate for the MFA program is approximately 6%, and the program has a strong track record of placing graduates in prestigious positions in the art world.

## 六、校友去向、展览机会、校园艺术氛围

UCSB has a vibrant art community, with numerous opportunities for students to exhibit their work and engage with the local and international art scene. The AD&A Museum, located on campus, provides a space for exhibitions and is an important resource for students and faculty.
