# External Quality Review 2021 - Summary

## Source Information
**Review**: MusiQuE/EQ-Arts Quality Enhancement Review  
**Date**: March 8-10, 2021 (virtual site visit due to COVID-19)  
**Programs Reviewed**: Bachelor and Master in Visual Arts (English and Dutch)  
**Type**: Independent external quality assessment  
**Reliability**: ✅ OFFICIAL - Highest reliability (third-party assessment)

## KEY VERIFIED FACTS

### Student Numbers (2019-20) ✅
- **Total Visual Arts students**: ~568 per year
- **Fashion**: ~200 students (largest specialism)
- **Graphic Design**: ~100 students (second largest)
- **International students**: 60% of total
- **Language split**: 50% English, 50% Dutch

**Breakdown by program**:
- Bachelor Visual Arts (English): 228 students
- Bachelor Visual Arts (Dutch): 224 students
- Master Visual Arts (English): 58 students
- Master Visual Arts (Dutch): 58 students

> **Source**: Quality Review Report, page 3-4, Student & Study Profile 2017-2020

## AREAS FOR IMPROVEMENT

### 1. Facilities & Equipment ⚠️

**Issue**: Shared technical spaces and equipment visibility, maintenance, and access processes need improvement

**Affected programs**: Particularly In Situ, Graphic Design, Fashion (not at main Mutsaard campus)

**Student feedback**: Alumni and current students mentioned limited access to equipment and unclear processes

> **Source**: Quality Review Report, Section 5.1 Facilities

**Review Team Recommendation**: Improve visibility and access to shared technical resources

---

### 2. Theory-Practice Integration ⚠️

**Issue**: Disconnect between theoretical studies and artistic practice

**Impact**: Contributing factor to sub-optimal Bachelor academic success rates

**Student feedback**: Some students found theory courses less relevant to their practice

**Review Team Recommendation**: Continue work to embed and improve relevance of theoretical studies to artistic practice

> **Source**: Quality Review Report, Section 2.1, 3.2

---

### 3. Employability & Professional Skills ⚠️

**Issue**: Limited preparation for professional life and business skills

**Student/Alumni feedback**:
- Lack of preparedness for professional world
- Insufficient focus on employability
- Limited tools for starting own business
- Structured elective courses (e.g., Arts Management) found less useful

**Positive**: Internships highly valued where available

**Review Team Recommendation**:
- Embed employability and professional skills incrementally from BA through MA
- Expand internship opportunities across all specialisms
- Collect alumni data annually to identify employment outcomes

> **Source**: Quality Review Report, Section 3.2

---

### 4. Cross-Studio Collaboration ⚠️

**Issue**: Limited opportunities for cross-atelier/studio collaboration

**Review Team Recommendation**: Strengthen collaboration between different specialisms

> **Source**: Quality Review Report, Section 2.1

## WHAT THIS MEANS FOR APPLICANTS

### ✅ Strengths to Expect
- Personalized mentorship and studio-based learning
- International, diverse student community (60% international)
- Strong connection to Antwerp arts scene
- Individual artistic development prioritized

### ⚠️ Areas to Be Aware Of
- **Equipment access**: May need to be proactive about accessing shared technical spaces (especially if not at main Mutsaard campus)
- **Professional preparation**: Limited structured business/employability training; may need to seek opportunities independently
- **Theory courses**: Some students find theory less relevant; integration improving

### 💡 Recommendations for Applicants
- **Seek internships**: Highly valued by students who had them; ask about opportunities in your specialism
- **Be proactive**: Equipment and professional development may require self-advocacy
- **Leverage location**: Take advantage of Antwerp's art scene and cultural institutions
