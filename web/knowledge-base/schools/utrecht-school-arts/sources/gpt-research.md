- HKU is a large, practice-oriented art school in the Netherlands, offering a range of programs in fine arts, design, music, theater, media, games, and art management.
- The school has a strong emphasis on interdisciplinary collaboration and practical education, with a focus on preparing students for the creative industries.
- HKU has approximately 4,400 undergraduate students and 250 postgraduate students, with around 50 incoming exchange students each year.

### Program Offerings
- HKU offers a variety of programs, with most undergraduate programs taught in Dutch and a limited number of programs taught in English.
- The English-taught programs include Bachelor of Fine Art and Bachelor of Music (Utrechts Conservatorium) with various specializations.
- Master's programs are more diverse, with options in Fine Art, Scenography, Music Design, Performance, and more.

### Admission Requirements
- HKU requires IELTS or TOEFL scores for English-taught programs, with specific requirements depending on the program level.
- Portfolio requirements vary by program, with Fine Art programs requiring a strong emphasis on creativity, cultural interest, and visual talent.
- Applications are submitted through Studielink, with interviews conducted via Zoom for selected candidates.

### Tuition and Scholarships
- Tuition fees vary depending on the student's nationality and the program, with non-EU/EEA students paying around €12,258 per year for most programs.
- The HKU Talent Grant offers a partial tuition waiver of €3,500 for eligible non-EU students without a Dutch degree.
- Scholarships are available but highly competitive, with limited information provided on the official website.

### Student Life and Facilities
- HKU has multiple campuses in Utrecht, Hilversum, and Amersfoort, with access to a range of facilities including libraries, workshops, and sports facilities.
- The school has a strong connection with local and international cultural institutions, offering students opportunities to engage with the local art scene.

### Career Outcomes

- The school has a mutual support platform for MFA students and alumni, offering networking opportunities and resources for career development.
- Graduates can access the Exposure platform, which showcases the work of alumni and provides a digital archive of student projects.

### Suitability for International Students
