## 学校概况 / Overview ✅

### Basic Information ✅
- **Founded**: 1648 as Royal Academy of Painting and Sculpture (Académie royale de peinture et de sculpture)
- **Current name**: 1817 (École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts)
- **Historical significance**: One of the world's oldest and most prestigious art academies
- **Current status**: Public administrative institution under French Ministry of Culture
- **PSL member**: Part of Paris Sciences et Lettres (PSL) University since 2010
- **Address**: 14 rue Bonaparte, 75006 Paris + 126 rue des Rosiers, 93400 Saint-Ouen

### Historical Milestones
- **1648**: Royal Academy of Painting and Sculpture founded by Cardinal Mazarin
- **1793**: Dissolved during French Revolution
- **1816**: Reopened at former Petits-Augustins monastery site
- **1817**: Renamed École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts
- **1863**: Management transferred from Academy to State
- **2001**: École nationale supérieure d'architecture de Paris-Malaquais established on campus
- **2008**: Saint-Ouen campus opened (1,000 sqm for sculpture, ceramics, casting)
- **2010**: Joined PSL University
- **2016**: Via Ferrata preparatory program established (25 students/year)
- **2017**: Awarded "Musée de France" status by Ministry of Culture

## 专业设置 / Programs

> ✅ **Verification Status**: VERIFIED. Atelier-based system, not traditional major divisions.

### Academic Structure ✅

**⚠️ IMPORTANT**: Beaux-Arts de Paris does NOT use traditional "major" divisions (e.g., Painting Department, Sculpture Department). Instead, it uses an **Atelier (workshop) system** where students choose a renowned artist's studio and work across media and disciplines.

**5-Year Program Structure**:
- **Years 1-3**: Premier Cycle (First Cycle) → Diplôme de premier cycle
- **Years 4-5**: Second Cycle → DNSAP (Diplôme national supérieur d'arts plastiques)
  - **DNSAP** = Master's level since 2012
- **Post-Diploma**: Optional 1-year transition (non-degree)
- **Doctoral**: SACRe program (PSL cross-institutional PhD)

### Core Components

**Artistic Practice** (Atelier):
- Student chooses an artist's workshop
- Cross-media, cross-disciplinary practice
- Painting, sculpture, installation, photography, video, performance, sound art

**Theory**:
- Art history
- Contemporary art theory
- Seminars and research workshops

**Technical Training**:
- Drawing, printmaking, painting
- Sculpture, casting, ceramics (Saint-Ouen campus)
- Photography, video, digital media

**Transversal Courses** (Diagonales):
- Cross-disciplinary projects
- Collaboration with other PSL institutions

### Two Campuses

**Paris Campus** (Saint-Germain-des-Prés):
- **Size**: 2+ hectares
- **Facilities**: Studios, 3 lecture halls, contemporary art library, 2 exhibition spaces
- **Exhibition spaces**: Palais des Beaux-Arts, Cabinet des dessins Jean Bonna
- **Collection**: 450,000+ artworks (second only to Louvre in France)

**Saint-Ouen Campus**:
- **Size**: 1,000 sqm (former ice rink, opened 2008)
- **Facilities**: Sculpture, mosaic, modeling, casting, ceramics, composite materials workshops
- **Via Ferrata program**: State-funded preparatory course (25 students/year, established 2016)

### Atelier System ✅
- **Mentor selection**: Teachers choose students, students choose teachers
- **40-50 workshops**: Students apply to specific artist's studio
- **Evaluation**: Not traditional grades - portfolio reviews, exhibitions, critiques
- **Doctoral evaluation**: Not thesis - photography exhibition, painting creation, etc.

### Campus Atmosphere ✅
- **French language environment**: All courses in French, high language requirement
- **Historical setting**: 17th-19th century buildings, rich art history context
- **Paris art scene**: Close to Louvre, galleries, museums, art institutions
- **PSL resources**: Access to cross-disciplinary courses and resources

### Teaching Style ✅
- **Studio-based**: Long-term, daily, sustained engagement
- **Artist-led**: Renowned artists as mentors
- **Theory + practice**: Art history, contemporary theory, technical skills
- **Exhibition-focused**: Regular student exhibitions, open studio days

### Career Paths ⚠️
- **Artists**: Independent artists, gallery representation
- **Teachers**: Art teachers, university professors
- **Art professionals**: Curators, art advisors, gallery owners
- **Cultural institutions**: Museums, cultural centers, public art projects

## Comparison with Peer Schools

### France Tier 1 Art Academies
- **ENSAD (École nationale supérieure des Arts Décoratifs)**: Design-focused (graphic, product, textile, animation, interior, fashion)
- **ENSAPC (École nationale supérieure d'arts de Paris-Cergy)**: New media, interaction, photography, digital design

### Advantages over ENSAD
- Stronger in pure fine arts (painting, sculpture, installation)
- Deeper historical collection and prestige
- More traditional atelier system
- Better for contemporary art practice

### Disadvantages vs. ENSAD
- Weaker in applied design (product, graphic, fashion)
- Less industry connections for commercial design
- More traditional, less innovation-focused

### Global Comparison
- **Florence Academy, Bologna Academy, Repin Academy**: Similar historical prestige, "World's Four Great Art Academies"
- **RCA (Royal College of Art)**: QS #1 vs Beaux-Arts #40, but RCA tuition £35,950/year vs Beaux-Arts 438 EUR/year
- **RISD, SAIC, CalArts**: Different style (more contemporary/experimental), much higher tuition
