# Institut National des Arts de Bamako

## TL;DR
The Institut National des Arts de Bamako (INA) is a prestigious national art school in Mali, established in 1933. It offers a range of programs in visual and performing arts, traditional crafts, and has a rich history of producing notable artists. The school has undergone several name changes and has faced challenges, including a significant fire in 2013, but remains a vital cultural institution in Mali.

## Key Claims
### Establishment and History
- **Established in 1933**: The INA was originally founded as the Ecole Artisanale du Soudan to train Sudanese artisans.
- **Name Changes**: The school has been known by various names, reflecting its evolution and the political changes in Mali, including Ecole Artisanale du Soudan, Maison des artisans du Soudan, Institut National des Arts, Maison des artisans du Mali, and finally Maison des Artisans de Bamako.
- **Focus**: The INA's focus has expanded from artisanal training to include a wide range of art forms.

### Programs and Courses
- **Artistic Offerings**: The INA offers courses in jewellery making and design, illustration, painting, sculpture, photography, music, and theatre.
- **Notable Alumni**: The school has produced many of Mali’s most well-known artists, including Yaya Coulibaly, Habib Dembélé, Ismael Diabate, Amahiguere Dolo, Habib Koité, Abdoulaye Konaté, Malick Sidibé, and Salif Traoré.

### Challenges and Impact
- **Fire in 2013**: A large fire destroyed many of the craftsmen's shops and wares in the surrounding area, impacting the school and local artisans.
- **Tourism and Craftsmanship**: The area surrounding the INA is a popular tourist destination, with over sixty thousand tourists visiting annually to view craftsmen working and to buy crafts from the market.
