### Video 4: Comprehensive Analysis (19 min) ✅

**Content**: Detailed analysis of UC Berkeley - environment, culture, colleges, admissions  
**Relevance to Art Practice**: General university context, admissions framework

**Key Points**:

**Safety** ⚠️:
- Neighbors Oakland (one of most dangerous US cities)
- Regularly on "most dangerous universities" lists
- School sends safety alert emails regularly
- Free "Bear Walk" night escort service
- Off-campus areas have homeless, drug users, robbery/assault risks

**Academic Culture**:
- **Founded 1868**: Mission to serve public good (vs Ivy League elite培养)
- **1964 Free Speech Movement**: Shaped culture of questioning authority, critical thinking
- **GPA deflation**: Many courses require avg GPA of B-/B (2.7-3.0), A limited to 15-17%
- **P/NP policy**: Can convert courses to Pass/No Pass (C+ = Pass), protects GPA

**Faculty Resources**:
- Top-tier professors (Nobel, Turing Award winners)
- CS faculty all from MIT, CMU, Stanford, Berkeley
- **URAP (Undergraduate Research Apprentice Program)**: Students must apply competitively like job applications

**Shortcomings**:
- **Mental health support insufficient**: Counseling center always overbooked, long waitlists
- High anxiety/depression rates due to intense competition

**College of Letters & Science** (where Art Practice is):
- Largest college, most Chinese students
- Popular majors: CS, Data Science, Economics, Applied Math
- **CS transfer risk**: Must complete 3 prerequisites, meet rising GPA threshold among thousands of competitors
- **L&S CS median salary**: $135K/year (vs EECS $129K)

**College of Engineering**:
- Highest academic intensity
- **EECS international acceptance rate**: 6%
- Requires AP Calc BC, Physics C, strong engineering background
- ~20% continue to grad school (vs L&S CS 12%)

**Haas School of Business**:
- Rare top business school with undergrad program
- Direct admission very difficult, most transfer in sophomore/junior year
- 4 principles: Question the Status Quo, Confidence Without Attitude, Beyond Yourself, Students Always
- Emphasizes case studies, teamwork, leadership

**Admissions**:
- **CA residents prioritized** (legal requirement)
- **Avg admitted GPA**: 4.22 (weighted)
- **NO SAT required**, **NO recommendation letters**
- **4 PIQs (Personal Insight Questions)**: 350 words each, only chance to show personality
- Focus: Course rigor + GPA, depth in specific field, impact, not quantity of activities
- Prefers实干型 (action-oriented), socially conscious applicants

**Core Philosophy**:
- Provides top resources + brutal competition, **NO hand-holding**
- All opportunities require proactive pursuit
- Survivors become independent problem-solvers

### Video 5: 5 Things to Know Before Attending (8 min) ✅

**Content**: UP主BRADY shares 5 essential facts for incoming students  
**Relevance to Art Practice**: General campus realities

**5 Key Points**:

**1. Safety** ⚠️:
- Neighbors Oakland (top 5 most dangerous US cities)
- Berkeley consistently in top 5-10 most dangerous universities
- **3-4 safety alert emails per week** during UP主's attendance (sexual harassment, assault, robbery)
- Free Bear Walk escort service available
- UP主 never walks alone at night, always takes Uber

**2. Housing** ⚠️:
- **Only guaranteed for freshmen**, dorm prices higher than market rate
- Sophomores+ must enter lottery for on-campus housing, not guaranteed
- Most students forced to rent off-campus
- **UP主 rent examples**: Sophomore shared 1BR $2,356/month; Junior private room in 5BR $850/month (20-min bus commute)
- Finding housing requires considering roommates, price, location, commute - time-consuming, start early

**3. GPA Deflation** ⚠️:
- **Opposite of Ivy League grade inflation** (Harvard/Yale/Brown most students get As)
- **Curve grading**: Most courses require avg GPA of B-/B (2.7-3.0), fixed percentage per grade
- Absolute score doesn't matter, only relative ranking vs classmates
- **Impact**: Students aiming for law/med school or finance need extra effort to maintain high GPA
- Public university must admit many CA residents, some lower-performing students lower curve

**4. Academic & Location Resources**:
- **Faculty**: CS professors all from MIT, CMU, Stanford, Berkeley; many cross-school PhDs
- **Labs**: Lawrence Lab (natural sciences, many internships for Berkeley students), top AI lab (CS)
- **Location**: East Bay, near Silicon Valley & SF, all major companies + unicorns at career fairs
- Exploring Bay Area early helps for post-grad employment in region

**5. Major Preparation**:
- **Workload varies greatly** by major: CS, Chemistry, Molecular Biology extremely demanding
- Research target major's curriculum before enrollment, prepare mentally
- Consider backup major (Plan B) if needed
