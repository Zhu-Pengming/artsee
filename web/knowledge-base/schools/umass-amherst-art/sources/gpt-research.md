# UMass Amherst Department of Art Comprehensive Research Report

## TL;DR
- The UMass Amherst Department of Art offers a range of programs including BA, BFA, MA, and MFA in Studio Art, with a focus on interdisciplinary study and strong faculty. The department is part of the College of Humanities & Fine Arts and benefits from the Five College Consortium. It is known for its high value for money, especially for MFA students who receive full funding. The report highlights the department's structure, programs, admissions, costs, facilities, faculty, and career outcomes, with a focus on its appeal to Chinese students.

## Key Findings

### Department Identity
- **Location**: UMass Amherst, College of Humanities & Fine Arts
- **Website**: [UMass Amherst Art Department](https://www.umass.edu/art/)
- **Chair**: Young Min Moon (Distinguished Professor, Korean-American painter and critic)

- **Interdisciplinary Approach**: The department emphasizes interdisciplinary study, with MFA and BFA General Track not tied to a single medium.

### Degree Portfolio
- **BA in Art**: Broad liberal-arts framing
- **BFA in Art & Design**: Seven studio concentrations (Animation, Ceramics, Design & Technology, Intermedia, Painting, Print Media, Sculpture)
- **BFA in Art Education**: Accredited by Massachusetts DESE and NCATE, includes K-12 student teaching
- **Minor in Art**: Competitive limited enrollment
- **Certificate in Design & Creative Technologies (DCT, 19 credits)**: Competitive limited enrollment
- **MA in Art Education**: 36 credits, ≥12 cr at 600-level
- **MFA in Studio Art**: 60 credits, 3 years, interdisciplinary, ~6 admits/year

### Admissions
- **Undergraduate**: Common App + SlideRoom portfolio, early action and regular decision deadlines, no GRE required
- **MFA**: UMass Graduate School application, SlideRoom portfolio, letters of reference, statement, community essay
- **MA Art Education**: Portfolio, letters of reference, art/art-history coursework, minimum undergrad GPA 2.75
- **International**: Accepts TOEFL, IELTS, and Duolingo; specific English proficiency requirements vary by program

### Cost
- **Undergraduate**: In-state tuition + fees ≈ $17,772; out-of-state ≈ $40,449; total COA in-state ≈ $37,122, out-of-state ≈ $59,799
- **Graduate**: In-state tuition ≈ $17,410 / out-of-state ≈ $35,726 per year; per-credit out-of-state grad ≈ $1,856
- **MFA Funding**: Fully-funded via teaching assistantships covering full tuition, fee waiver, stipend, and health benefits
