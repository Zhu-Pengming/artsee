# University of Waterloo

## TL;DR
The University of Waterloo is a public research university in Waterloo, Ontario, Canada, known for its strong engineering and co-operative education programs. Established in 1959, the university has grown to become a leading institution in Canada and globally, with a strong focus on research, innovation, and sustainability. The university offers a wide range of academic programs across six faculties, and its co-operative education program is the largest in the world. The university has a diverse student body and a rich history of notable alumni, including Nobel laureates and successful entrepreneurs.

### Establishment and History
- **Waterloo College Associate Faculties**: The university originated from the Waterloo College Associate Faculties, which were established in 1956 as a semi-autonomous entity of Waterloo College, an affiliate of the University of Western Ontario. The Associate Faculties were established to meet the need for engineers and technicians in Canada's postwar economy.
- **University of Waterloo Act**: The University of Waterloo Act was passed by the Legislative Assembly of Ontario in 1959, formally splitting the Associated Faculties from Waterloo College and re-establishing it as the University of Waterloo.
- **Growth and Expansion**: The university has grown significantly since its establishment, adding new faculties, programs, and research centers. It has also expanded its physical footprint, with additional campuses in Cambridge, Stratford, and Kitchener.

### Campus and Location
- **Main Campus**: The main campus is located in Waterloo, Ontario, Canada, with a total area of 404 hectares. The campus includes a variety of buildings and facilities, including academic buildings, research centers, and student residences.
