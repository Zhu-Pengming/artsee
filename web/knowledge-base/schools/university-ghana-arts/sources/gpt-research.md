# Research Report: Arts Education at the University of Ghana (Legon)

## TL;DR

This comprehensive report provides an in-depth overview of arts education at the University of Ghana (UG), focusing on the School of Performing Arts (SPA). It offers a detailed analysis of the institution's history, academic programs, admission requirements, costs, and the international student experience. The report also compares UG's arts programs with other institutions in Africa and highlights key considerations for Chinese families considering study abroad opportunities in Ghana.

## Key Claims

### 1. University of Ghana Background

- UG is the oldest, largest, and most prestigious university in Ghana, with a strong international reputation.
- UG has around 38,000-40,000 students and welcomes more than 1,500 international students from over 70 countries.
- The university is organized on a collegiate system with four colleges, including the College of Humanities, which houses the SPA.
- UG has a rich history of producing notable alumni and has been recognized by various global rankings.

### 2. Arts Education at UG

- The University of Ghana does not have a unified "School of Art" but offers arts disciplines across the College of Humanities.
- The School of Performing Arts (SPA) is the primary arts unit, offering programs in music, dance studies, and theatre arts.
- Visual fine art is not a major degree offering at UG; students seeking BFA in those disciplines typically attend KNUST instead.
- SPA is known for its pioneering academic discipline in African music, dance, and theatre studies.

### 3. Academic Programs

- SPA offers diploma, BA, BFA, MA, MFA, MPhil, and PhD programs in music, dance studies, and theatre arts.
- The undergraduate programs include diploma, BA, BFA, and B.Mus. degrees, with standard duration of four years.
- Graduate programs range from one-year MA to two-year MFA and PhD.
- The curriculum emphasizes the integration of African performance traditions with Western/global theatre, music, and dance practices.

### 4. Admissions Information

- UG accepts various international qualifications, including Cambridge GCE 'A' Levels, International Baccalaureate, and American High School Diplomas.
- English language proficiency is required, and students from non-English-medium systems may need to complete a one-year Certificate in English Proficiency.
- Admissions to SPA programs require an audition, interview, or portfolio to assess creative potential.
- The application process involves submitting an application through UG's portal and paying an application fee.

### 5. Tuition and Costs

- UG charges international students in US dollars, with two tiers: African nationals and non-African nationals.
- The estimated tuition for a non-African BFA/BA/B.Mus. student is roughly US$5,100-5,500 per year.
- Graduate tuition varies depending on the program, with a one-year MA in Theatre Arts costing around US$7,900.
- Accommodation fees range from GHS 2,000 to GHS 8,000 per academic year, with international hostels being more expensive.
- Living costs in Accra range from GHS 500 per month for modest local living to US$300-500 per month for a comfortable lifestyle.

### 6. International Student Experience

- Chinese citizens require a visa to enter Ghana and must apply for a student visa at the Ghana Embassy in Beijing.
- UG's International Programmes Office (IPO) provides support services for visas, accommodation, orientation, residence permits, and welfare.
- The Confucius Institute at the University of Ghana offers language courses, cultural events, and HSK testing for Chinese students.
- The Chinese Students Association organizes cultural events and provides a platform for Chinese students to connect with each other.
- Ghana is generally regarded as a safe country, and UG explicitly advises students to take normal urban precautions.

### 7. UGC and Community Perspectives

- The School of Performing Arts is widely regarded as the leading academic centre for African music, dance, and theatre studies on the continent.
- UG's arts programs are compared with those of other institutions like UCT, KNUST, and Makerere University.
- UG's unique academic content, affordability, and strong on-campus support make it an attractive option for Chinese families.
