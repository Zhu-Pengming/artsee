# Purdue University 艺术相关学院 · 选校决策 Wiki

## TL;DR
- **Purdue West Lafayette · Rueff School** 定位于 Big Ten 研究型大学内的 BFA 设计学院，对预算敏感的中国家庭具备性价比，但艺术圈品牌、都市资源、STEM-OPT 资格均不如 RISD/Parsons/Pratt 与 Cincinnati DAAP。
- **Purdue Fort Wayne** 与 West Lafayette 完全独立，仅适合明确想读特定音乐或视觉艺术项目、且接受地方型学位的学生。
- **关键警告**：Rueff School 的设计相关项目不在 DHS STEM 名单中，中国学生毕业后只有 12 个月标准 OPT，无 24 月 STEM 延期。
- **Purdue West Lafayette** 适合希望背靠强工科生态、读 CIDA 认证室内设计、看重 Big Ten 校园文化、希望从工业设计延伸到 UX/Interaction Design 研究生的学生。
- **Purdue Fort Wayne** 适合想读 BS Music Therapy / BFA Musical Theatre、地方型 BFA 视觉艺术、且接受非主校品牌的学生。

## Key Claims

### Purdue West Lafayette · Rueff School

- **定位与排名**：Big Ten 研究型大学内的 BFA 设计学院，综合大学排名 1.5 档，艺术学院排名 2.5 档。
- **适合人群**：想读 Industrial Design BFA、CIDA 认证室内设计 BFA、预算敏感、看重 Big Ten 校园文化、希望从工业设计延伸到 UX/Interaction Design 研究生的学生。
- **不适合人群**：追求 STEM OPT 24 个月延期、想读独立艺术学院级别 Fine Arts、看重大城市艺术生态、想读 BMus Music Performance 本科、不接受“大二二次选拔”机制的学生。
- **相似项目学校**：Ohio State Design、University of Cincinnati DAAP、Virginia Tech School of Architecture + Design 等。
- **优势**：工科赋能的产品/交互设计、公立性价比、Big Ten 校园体验、CIDA Interior Design + 综合大学组合、研究生 Interaction Design（小班 + TA 资助）。
- **劣势**：纯艺声誉与展览资源、时尚/插画/动画在艺术圈认可度、地理位置与都市艺术资源、中国留学家庭品牌辨识度。

### Purdue Fort Wayne

- **定位与排名**：地方型综合大学，艺术与表演艺术学院。
- **适合人群**：想读 BS Music Therapy / BFA Musical Theatre、地方型 BFA 视觉艺术、且接受非主校品牌的学生。
- **不适合人群**：希望毕业拿 "Purdue University" 学位证、想要研究型大学/强工科加持、追求中文留学圈认知度、想要丰富都市艺术资源/ networking 的学生。
- **相似学校**：Ball State CFA、IU South Bend Raclin、Western Michigan 等。
- **优势**：BS Music Therapy（AMTA 体系）、BFA Musical Theatre（NASAT）、整体性价比。
- **劣势**：综合声誉/研究资源/国际生群体/品牌识别度。

### STEM/OPT 警告

- Rueff School 的设计相关项目不在 DHS STEM 名单中，中国学生毕业后只有 12 个月标准 OPT，无 24 月 STEM 延期。

## Notes/Analysis

- Purdue West Lafayette 和 Purdue Fort Wayne 是完全独立的学校，各自有独立的招生、学位、排名体系。
- Rueff School 的设计相关项目不在 DHS STEM 名单中，对中国学生毕业后OPT的影响需要特别注意。
- Purdue West Lafayette 的地理位置相对偏远，艺术资源相对有限，但 Big Ten 校园文化和工科背景是其优势。
- Purdue Fort Wayne 的音乐治疗和音乐剧项目是其特色，但整体规模和声誉相对较弱。

### Student Experience

- **Purdue West Lafayette**
  - Housing: University Residences (93% of freshman live on campus); off-campus apartments $480–$900/month
  - Commute: CityBus ($1 per ride, $50/month), walking, 1 hour to Indianapolis, 2 hours to Chicago
  - Safety: Purdue campus + West Lafayette often listed among the safest universities in the U.S.
  - Student life: Greek life, 1,000+ clubs, football + basketball + Grand Prix go-kart, CSSA
  - Workload: hands-on focus, tight industry connection, model-making and corporate-sponsored projects
  - Faculty support: core faculty include Dr. Cheryl Zhenyu Qian, Steve Visser, TJ Kim, Jung Joo Sohn
  - Studio resources: Pao Hall facilities, 24-hour access after sophomore review, Bechtel Innovation Design Center
  - Funding: MFA ID/IXD provide TA (tuition remission + stipend), limited merit aid for undergraduate international students
  - Career: Big Ten Career Fair, ID graduates work at Hasbro, International Trucks, Whirlpool, Delta Faucet, etc.
  - Portfolio application: Integrated Studio Arts BFA requires 10–15 images, uploaded through Purdue application portal
  - International student experience: ISS and CSSA systems mature, often described as "Purdue village, simple, focused on academics"
  - Chinese student perspective: Purdue = "engineering school / public Ivy League / Deng Xiaoping alumnus"; Rueff = "engineering + business + agriculture + art" at the same school, Big Ten Career Fair strong, but art "bloodline" and urban resources weak
  - City art resources: West Lafayette + Lafayette (10万人口), main art resources on campus, local art institutions limited, 2 hours to Chicago Art Institute / SAIC, 1 hour to Indianapolis Museum of Art

- **Purdue Fort Wayne**
  - Housing: Niche / Sallie — "top-tier" student dorms, most students commute; off-campus rent significantly lower than West Lafayette
  - Commute: Fort Wayne public transportation weak; most students drive
  - Safety: average city level; campus relatively safe
  - Student life: Niche — Greek life weak, limited club base; basketball + volleyball are main sports culture; CSSA/International Student Organization small
  - Workload / faculty support / studio resources: Niche student reviews (few) — "professors take the time to get to know students individually"
  - Funding: 99% of students receive some form of aid, average aid $6,589; international students mainly rely on merit + program scholarships
  - Career: Niche / Sallie — median starting salary $44,088 (far lower than West Lafayette); regional employment network; PFW own case study (B.A. '16 Brady Schrock at Shanghai Disney Resort as Jack Sparrow)
  - Portfolio application: BFA Musical Theatre video audition; BFA Fine Arts/VCD portfolio review after BA level
  - International student experience: international students ~2%; Office of International Education provides advising / immigration / cross-cultural
  - Chinese student perspective: Gap: No enough Chinese UGC evidence; PFW has almost no original content by international students on Xiaohongshu / Zhihu / Bilibili; Chinese education agency pages also rarely recommend PFW
  - City art resources (Fort Wayne): Fort Wayne Museum of Art, Fort Wayne Philharmonic, Embassy Theatre, Fort Wayne Civic Theatre — slightly more than West Lafayette (26万人口 city), but far less than Chicago/NYC/LA

### Decision Notes

- **Purdue West Lafayette**
  - Purdue in Chinese families = "engineering school / public Ivy League / Deng Xiaoping alumnus"; Rueff = "engineering + business + agriculture + art" at the same school, Big Ten Career Fair strong, but art "bloodline" and urban resources weak
  - **Purdue Fort Wayne**
  - PFW ≠ Purdue main campus; degree certificate, enrollment, academic affairs, ranking are independent. If the family wants "Purdue degree + low cost," they must read West Lafayette.
  - PFW's true strengths: BS Music Therapy (AMTA system), BFA Musical Theatre (NASAT), overall affordability
  - STEM/OPT: PFW art programs are also not on the DHS STEM CIP list, international students have only 12 months standard OPT, no 24-month STEM extension
  - Brand recognition: Chinese HR and Chinese education market have very low awareness of PFW; return-to-work only relies on the "Purdue" name on the resume for a limited benefit

### Recommendations

- **Stage 1 (Grade 10/11, Planning Phase)**
  - If the family clearly wants STEM-OPT, **Purdue Rueff is not the first choice**. Prioritize Cincinnati DAAP (co-op + multiple STEM-coded design projects), CMU Design, UIUC iSchool/HCI path, or transfer to HCI/Interaction Design graduate programs.
  - If the family accepts a non-STEM path, list Rueff as a **target school**, focus on preparing GPA, TOEFL ≥ 100, Common App essay, and Why-Purdue.
  - Simultaneously prepare a 10–15 image portfolio (whether applying for Integrated Studio Arts BFA or not, it is material for sophomore review preparation).

- **Stage 2 (First Semester of Grade 12)**
  - Apply to Purdue (Nov 1 EA) and lock in Trustees/Presidential Scholarship evaluation; BFA Integrated Studio Arts submit 10–15 images through Purdue application portal simultaneously.
  - Simultaneously apply to at least 1 RISD/Parsons/Pratt/CCS-like portfolio application, and 1 Cincinnati DAAP/Ohio State Design-like design school with engineering support — use Comparable Schools for cross-sectional comparison.

- **Stage 3 (After receiving offers)**
  - When comparing Purdue Rueff offer vs. other art school offers, evaluate based on **4 weights**: (1) STEM-OPT eligibility, (2) program-level placement data, (3) 4-year total COA, (4) geographic art resources.
